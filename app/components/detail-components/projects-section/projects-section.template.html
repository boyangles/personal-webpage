We streamed live data from the Duke University Smart Home to develop a machine learning algorithm to classify malfunctioning home-appliances.

With historical data, we trained our algorithm to conduct forecasting and anomaly detection.

Further details of the project can be found here. Take a look at our data toolkit on Github here as well as our poster results here.

In the summer of 2016, I developed software for the GS Compliance Division. I created solutions to interpret harmful electronic communications sent and received via the GS Network in order to mitigate risk across the firm.

The solutions process millions of messages a day, so I designed a multithreaded extraction process to schedule interpreted retrievals. I also created a web-based dashboard to help users interpret the retrieved data.
